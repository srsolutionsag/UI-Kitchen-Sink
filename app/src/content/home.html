About the Kitchen Sink

The UI-Kitchen-Sinks represents the specification, classification, documentation and illustration of UI-Elements in ILIAS. It should help:

  • Developers to find the right UI-Element for their need
  • Customers to check for available resources to fullfill their needs
  • Testers to check if a feature is implemented according to UI-Guidelines
  • End-Users to have a more streamlined user experience with ILIAS

Terminology

In the UI-Kitchen-Sink a lot of existing terms of ILIAS-UI-Items have been reused. However there are some additions worth explaining as well:

Elements
Elements describe basic functionalities or semantic classes. They are often combined together in collections. Examples are single form input elements, fonts, colors, buttons etc.
Collections
Collections describe combinations of elements. They mostly form tools (such as calendars, info-boxes, forms and tables) that are reused in different places of ILIAS.
View
A view is an arrangement of collections and elements and describes a complete functionality in ILIAS (such as the permissions screen or the personal desktop)
Layouts
Layouts describe ways to arrange elements and tools to form a complete view.
